# The Sword in His Hand
*Vision-casting talk for Trail Life MN-9871 Leadership Night — May 2026*
*Spoken time: ~12 to 15 minutes*

---

## Open: The moment that changed how I see this.

A while back I sat at a table with a group of dads and their 13-year-old sons. We had steak and eggs. We talked about what it means to become a man. And then one by one, each boy knelt, and each dad placed a forged sword in his son's hand.

Not symbolic. A real sword. Cold steel. Weight in the palm.

And I watched these boys stand up different than they sat down.

Not because of the sword. Because of the dad standing behind them.

Because a man they loved looked them in the eye and said: *You are ready. I believe in you. Go.*

That is what we are doing here. Every Thursday. Scaled differently, maybe. But the same thing.

We are the dads behind the boys. And tonight I need you with me.

---

## The vision: what we're building.

Trail Life is not a program. It is a covenant. We said at the beginning that this troop exists to raise young men who treasure Christ, who lead their families, who know how to work with their hands and their hearts. Men who can look a son in the eye someday and say the same thing those dads said.

That does not happen by accident. It happens because men showed up on Thursday nights when they were tired. It happens because somebody drove the van, ran the campout, remembered the gear, led the devotional, and stayed until 9:30 to stack the chairs.

It happens because of what you do in this room.

---

## The ask: one year, one role.

I am not asking for a lifetime. I am asking for August 2026 to May 2027.

One season. One role. Faithfully.

Every role in this troop has a job description. Not bureaucracy. Clarity. So you know exactly what you are signing up for, who you report to, and what success looks like. No guessing.

Tonight you are going to pull out your phone, scan a QR code, and pick your role. You will see every spot on the board. You will see your brothers filling them in real time. And by the end of this meeting I want every card green.

Not because I need it to look good. Because these boys need it to be true.

---

## The weight of it.

I want to be honest with you. This troop is growing. That is a gift and it is a responsibility. We have boys in this room who do not have a dad at home who will ever put a sword in their hand. We have boys who will only hear "I believe in you" if one of you says it.

That is not guilt. That is an invitation. You have something to give that money cannot buy and programming cannot replicate.

Your presence. Your consistency. Your voice.

---

## Close: pick up the sword.

Those boys at the table. When the ceremony was over, when the fathers stepped back, the swords stayed in the sons' hands. That is the whole point. We are not raising boys who need us forever. We are raising men who will someday do this for their own sons.

But it starts with us. Tonight. In this room.

Pick your role. Show up. The boys are watching.

*Let's go.*
